Background & Significance of the study



The value of composting to communities and the environment is that compost can help keep the community and environment clean, due to its natural components. Based on research, gardeners and farmers add compost to the soil to improve the physical properties of the plant and soil due to it having a more reliable and sustainable source. Compost reduces and eliminates the need for chemical fertilizers because it is more accessible and more eco-friendly than chemical fertilizers, as chemical fertilizers usually worsen the soil. Therefore, compost is a better substitute for plant growth than chemical fertilizers.

Our partner institution can make use/benefit from composting in general, by allowing them to know that composting is better in order to plant vegetables. We say so because based on research and information given to us, compost can help provide nutrients that the plants need naturally, enrich the soil, and suppress plant diseases and pests. Talikala Inc. also benefits from this project by having a reliable and sustainable source of nutrients/food. Therefore, composting can really help our partner institution, if they ever want to start gardening or sell vegetables, then this is the best way to start.



Statement of the problem


Based on research, commercial fertilizers contain dangerous chemicals/ingredients that are considered toxic for the plants and us, they can harm the plants, and therefore, can also affect our environment harmfully by causing soil erosion, climate change, and environmental pollution (air and water pollution). It is also very expensive and sometimes difficult to find. So we figured, what would happen if instead of using commercial fertilizer, we use anaerobic composting for our plants? Composting helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions that affect/result in climate change and air pollution, and its also harmful to us if we are in the range of greenhouse gasses. What would happen if instead of using commercial fertilizer, we use anaerobic composting for our plants? We will experiment with this by using spring onion as our main plant, the type of compost used is Anaerobic Composting, and the type of fertilizer used will be complete fertilizer, with 14% Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium. Our independent variables will be the presence of Anaerobic composting and fertilizer. Our dependent variable will be the growth rate of the spring onion (stem and leaves).

Hypothesis

If we use Anaerobic Compost instead of complete fertilizer, then it can help improve the growth rate of the spring onion’s stem and leaves.


CONTROLLED
SET-UP

The control set-up contains 1 stalk/1 pc. of spring onion/onion, the soil must be between 5.3 and 5.8 pH of soil. The type of soil needed is humus-rich soil. The spring onions will be watered (2.5ml) every day, each week of theexperiment. It takes 8 weeks to grow the controlled set-up. This does not contain fertilizer or compost.


EXPERIMENTAL
SET-UP 1

The Experimental setup will contain 1 stalk of spring. The soil must be the humus-rich type and must be between 5.3 and 5.8 pH. The spring onions will be watered (2.5ml) every week of the experiment. We will measure the height of this set-up for 8 weeks. The difference between this setup and the controlled setup is that it will have is complete fertilizer (Ramgo brand) sprinkled on the soil.


EXPERIMENTAL
SET-UP 2

Experimental setup 2 will contain 1 stalk of spring onion. It will be planted in a plastic container filled with humus-rich soil, and biodegradable waste. The spring onions will be watered (2.5ml) and will measure their height for 8 weeks. The difference between this set-up compared to the control set-up and experimental set-up 1, is that it will contain compost applied above on the soil.
